ABOUT

Driven by sport & academics

Elijah Phiri (b. 1997, Lusaka) is a dedicated sports educator and coach with a strong academic foundation. He holds a Bachelor of Education in Physical Education & Sports from Kwame Nkrumah University and is currently pursuing a Master's in Social Work (MSW) and Project Management (PMME). His academic research focuses on the impact of school sports on youth mental health.

Elijah's sporting journey began as a student-athlete, representing his province in football and athletics. He later transitioned into coaching and teaching, earning certifications from the Football Association of Zambia (FAZ) and the International Handball Federation (IHF). As a volunteer with the Olympic Values Education Program (OVEP), he has reached over 1,500 children across Zambia, promoting respect, friendship, and excellence through sport.

His academic achievements include diplomas in Events Management, Sales Management, and Food Safety, as well as certificates in HIV/AIDS Awareness and Training of Trainers. Elijah believes that sport is a powerful tool for social change and continues to mentor young coaches in underprivileged communities.
